\subsection{The Dual-Ledger First Law}
To derive the Dark Energy equation of state ($w$) without double-counting energy, we explicitly partition the universe into two coupled thermodynamic ledgers: the \textbf{Vacuum Lattice} ($\mathcal{M}_A$) and the \textbf{Photon Gas} (CMB).

During the phase transition from the unstructured pre-geometric state into the discrete lattice, Lattice Genesis creates physically new volumetric space. Let the strictly constant baseline structural rest-energy density of this space be $\rho_{vac}$ (geometrically locked by the invariant $\kappa_V = 8\pi\alpha$ derived in Chapter 1). The internal energy required to fund the lattice itself scales strictly with the new volume ($dU_{vac} = \rho_{vac} dV$). 

Crucially, like any fluid freezing into a crystal, the phase transition of Lattice Genesis is exothermic. It continuously expels a Latent Heat of Fusion ($\rho_{latent} dV$) directly into the ambient photon gas of the universe. 

By the First Law of Thermodynamics, if the vacuum creates structural volume \textit{and} simultaneously expels thermal heat, it must thermodynamically fund \textit{both} from its own macroscopic mechanical expansion work ($P_{tot} dV$). The total energy balance for the active vacuum subsystem is:
\begin{equation}
    -P_{tot} dV = dU_{vac} + dQ_{latent\_out}
\end{equation} 
Substituting $dU_{vac} = \rho_{vac} dV$ and $dQ_{latent\_out} = \rho_{latent} dV$:
\begin{equation}
    -P_{tot} dV = \rho_{vac} dV + \rho_{latent} dV \implies P_{tot} = -(\rho_{vac} + \rho_{latent})
\end{equation}
