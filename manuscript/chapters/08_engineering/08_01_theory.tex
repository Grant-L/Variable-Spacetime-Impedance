\section{Metric Refraction: The Non-Geometric Warp}
\label{sec:metric_refraction}

\citestart SVF replaces the abstract geometric "warping" of spacetime with the mechanical \textbf{Refraction of Flux}[cite: 431]. A region of modified impedance $Z_{local}$ relative to the background $Z_0$ creates a local \textbf{Refractive Index} ($\chi$):

\begin{equation}
    \chi = \frac{Z_{local}}{Z_{0}} = \sqrt{\frac{L'_{node} C'_{node}}{L_{node} C_{node}}}
    \label{eq:refractive_index}
\end{equation}

\citestart When $\chi < 1$, the local group velocity $v_g$ exceeds the background speed of light $c$[cite: 434]. \citestart This creates a "Lattice Slip" zone, allowing for apparent superluminal translation relative to an external observer while remaining locally sub-saturating[cite: 435].

\subsection{The Lattice Stress Coefficient ($\sigma$)}
\citestart The magnitude of impedance modification is governed by the \textbf{Lattice Stress Coefficient} ($\sigma$), induced by high-frequency toroidal flux[cite: 441]. \citestart As $\sigma \to 1$, the node approaches total saturation, effectively "stiffening" the metric[cite: 442]. \citestart A critical engineering constraint is the \textbf{Impedance Mismatch} at the boundary of a stress bubble, which can trigger \textbf{Cherenkov Radiation} if the transition gradient is not properly tapered[cite: 443].
