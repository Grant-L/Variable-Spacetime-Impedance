\subsection{The Matched Constitutive Tensors}
We propose that the vacuum dielectric properties are coupled. A gravitational potential does not merely stiffen the lattice (Inductance); it simultaneously increases its charge storage density (Capacitance).

We define the Metric Deformation Tensor $\mathbf{D}_{ij}$ for a spherically symmetric mass $M$:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{D} = \begin{pmatrix} (1 - r_s/r)^{-1} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & (1 - r_s/r)^{-1} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & (1 - r_s/r)^{-1} \end{pmatrix}
\end{equation}

The Constitutive Tensors for the vacuum are:
\begin{align}
\mathbf{L}_{ij} &= L_0 \cdot \mathbf{D}_{ij} \\
\mathbf{C}_{ij} &= C_0 \cdot \mathbf{D}_{ij}
\end{align}

\subsection{Observational Verification}
\textbf{1. Variable Speed of Light (Gravity):}
The Refractive Index $n$ is determined by the product of the tensor components:
\begin{equation}
n_{ij} = c \sqrt{\mathbf{L}_{ij} \mathbf{C}_{ij}} = c \sqrt{L_0 C_0} \cdot \mathbf{D}_{ij}
\end{equation}
This produces the localized slowing of light required by General Relativity ($n > 1$).

\textbf{2. Constant Impedance (No Birefringence):}
The Characteristic Impedance $Z$ is determined by the ratio:
\begin{equation}
Z_{ij} = \sqrt{\frac{\mathbf{L}_{ij}}{\mathbf{C}_{ij}}} = \sqrt{\frac{L_0 \mathbf{D}_{ij}}{C_0 \mathbf{D}_{ij}}} = \sqrt{\frac{L_0}{C_0}} = Z_0 \approx 377 \Omega
\end{equation}
Because the deformation term $\mathbf{D}_{ij}$ cancels out, the Impedance remains a scalar invariant. Light of all polarizations travels at the same speed (determined by $n$) and encounters the same resistance ($Z$).
