\section{The Chiral Exclusion Principle}
\label{sec:chiral_exclusion}

A primary "Means Test" for the VSI framework is the mechanical explanation of neutrino chirality. \citestart While the Standard Model treats the absence of right-handed neutrinos as a broken symmetry, VSI identifies it as an \textbf{Impedance-Driven Attenuation}[cite: 1011, 1012].

\subsection{Impedance Clamping}
Recall the \textbf{Chiral Bias Equation} from Chapter 1. The manifold possesses an intrinsic orientation $\mathbf{\Omega}_{vac}$. \citestart When a topological twist ($h$) is introduced[cite: 1013, 1014]:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Left-Handed Helicity ($h < 0$):} Aligns with $\mathbf{\Omega}_{vac}$, encountering baseline impedance $Z_0$. The signal propagates freely.
    \item \textbf{Right-Handed Helicity ($h > 0$):} Opposes $\mathbf{\Omega}_{vac}$, triggering a non-linear impedance spike ($Z \rightarrow \infty$). \citestart This effectively clamps the signal[cite: 1015, 990].
\end{itemize}

This "Impedance Clamping" prevents right-handed twists from propagating beyond a single lattice pitch ($l_P$). \citestart Consequently, the right-handed neutrino is not "missing"; it is \textbf{Hardware Forbidden}[cite: 1016, 1017].
