\section{The Chiral Exclusion Principle}
\label{sec:chiral_exclusion}

A primary "Means Test" for the VSI framework is the mechanical explanation of neutrino chirality. \citestart While the Standard Model treats the absence of right-handed neutrinos as a broken symmetry, VSI identifies it as an \textbf{Impedance-Driven Attenuation}[cite: 1011, 1012].

\subsection{Impedance Clamping}
The vacuum manifold possesses an intrinsic orientation $\mathbf{\Omega}_{vac}$ (Lattice Grain). When a topological twist ($h$) propagates:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Left-Handed Helicity ($h < 0$):} Aligns with $\mathbf{\Omega}_{vac}$. The node impedance remains at baseline $Z_0 \approx 377 \Omega$. The signal propagates freely.
    \item \textbf{Right-Handed Helicity ($h > 0$):} Opposes $\mathbf{\Omega}_{vac}$. \citestart This conflict triggers a non-linear impedance spike ($Z \rightarrow \infty$), effectively "clamping" the signal[cite: 1015, 990].
\end{itemize}

This \textbf{Impedance Clamping} prevents right-handed twists from propagating beyond a single lattice pitch ($l_P$). \citestart Consequently, the right-handed neutrino is not "missing"; it is \textbf{Hardware Forbidden}[cite: 1016, 1017].
