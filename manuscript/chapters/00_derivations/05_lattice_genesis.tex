\subsection{The Dual-Ledger Balance Sheet}
To resolve the contradiction between the adiabatic pressure of Dark Energy and the exothermic latent heat required to power the CMB, we explicitly partition the universe into two coupled thermodynamic ledgers: the \textbf{Vacuum Lattice} ($\mathcal{M}_A$) and the \textbf{Photon Gas}.

During the phase transition from the pre-geometric state into the discrete lattice, Lattice Genesis creates new volumetric space. Let the constant baseline structural energy density of this space be $\rho_{vac}$ (geometrically locked by the invariant packing fraction $\kappa_V$). The internal energy of the lattice itself scales strictly with volume ($dU_{vac} = \rho_{vac} dV$). 

Crucially, the latent heat of this phase transition ($\rho_{latent} dV$) is expelled entirely out of the lattice and into the photon gas. By the First Law of Thermodynamics, if the vacuum creates volume \textit{and} expels heat, it must fund both from its own mechanical expansion work ($P_{tot} dV$). The total energy balance for the vacuum subsystem is:
\begin{equation}
-P_{tot} dV = dU_{vac} + dQ_{latent\_out}
\end{equation} 
Substituting $dU_{vac} = \rho_{vac} dV$ and $dQ_{latent\_out} = \rho_{latent} dV$:
\begin{equation}
-P_{tot} dV = \rho_{vac} dV + \rho_{latent} dV \implies P_{tot} = -(\rho_{vac} + \rho_{latent})
\end{equation} 
This mathematically proves that the mechanical pressure of the vacuum must be strictly \textit{more negative} than $-\rho_{vac}$ to balance the ledger. Calculating the equation of state natively derives \textbf{Phantom Dark Energy}:
\begin{equation}
w_{vac} = \frac{P_{tot}}{\rho_{vac}} = \frac{-(\rho_{vac} + \rho_{latent})}{\rho_{vac}} = -1 - \frac{\rho_{latent}}{\rho_{vac}}
\end{equation}
