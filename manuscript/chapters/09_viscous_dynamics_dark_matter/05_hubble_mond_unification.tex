\section{Deriving MOND from Shear-Thinning Vacuum Dynamics}
\label{sec:hubble_mond_unification}

In previous formulations of Modified Newtonian Dynamics (MOND), the acceleration threshold $a_0 \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m/s}^2$ is an empirical free parameter. In DCVE, we completely eliminate $a_0$ by deriving the flat rotation curve strictly from the visco-kinematics of the shear-thinning vacuum.

\subsection{The Non-Linear Poisson Equation (AQUAL)}
If the vacuum acts as a non-Newtonian shear-thinning fluid (as established in Section 9.1), the flat rotation curve emerges natively as the mathematical solution to the boundary layer without any circular kinematic postulates.

Let the vacuum's effective gravitational permeability $\mu_g$ depend non-linearly on the magnitude of the gravitational gradient $|\nabla \Phi|$ relative to a critical lattice acceleration floor $a_{genesis}$ (the kinematic drift of expansion):
\begin{equation}
    \mu_g(|\nabla \Phi|) = \frac{|\nabla \Phi|}{|\nabla \Phi| + a_{genesis}}
\end{equation}
The modified Poisson equation for the fluid stress becomes:
\begin{equation}
    \nabla \cdot \left( \mu_g(|\nabla \Phi|) \nabla \Phi \right) = 4\pi G \rho
\end{equation}
Integrating over a spherically symmetric galactic bulge of mass $M$ using Gauss's Theorem:
\begin{equation}
    \mu_g(|\nabla \Phi|) |\nabla \Phi| = \frac{GM}{r^2}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Asymptotic Fluid Limits}

\textbf{Regime I: High Shear (Inner Galaxy, $|\nabla \Phi| \gg a_{genesis}$)} \\
The permeability $\mu_g \to 1$. The equation reduces exactly to standard Newtonian gravity: $|\nabla \Phi| = \frac{GM}{r^2}$. The system exhibits standard Keplerian rotation ($v \propto r^{-1/2}$).

\textbf{Regime II: Low Shear (Outer Galaxy, $|\nabla \Phi| \ll a_{genesis}$)} \\
The permeability simplifies to $\mu_g \approx \frac{|\nabla \Phi|}{a_{genesis}}$. The fluid stress equation becomes:
\begin{equation}
    \left( \frac{|\nabla \Phi|}{a_{genesis}} \right) |\nabla \Phi| \approx \frac{GM}{r^2} \implies |\nabla \Phi|^2 = \frac{G M a_{genesis}}{r^2} \implies |\nabla \Phi| = \frac{\sqrt{G M a_{genesis}}}{r}
\end{equation}
Because the centripetal acceleration for a stable circular orbit is $v^2/r = |\nabla \Phi|$, we solve for the orbital velocity:
\begin{equation}
    \frac{v^2}{r} = \frac{\sqrt{G M a_{genesis}}}{r} \implies v^2 = \sqrt{G M a_{genesis}} 
\end{equation}

    v_{flat} = (G M a_{genesis})^{1/4}
