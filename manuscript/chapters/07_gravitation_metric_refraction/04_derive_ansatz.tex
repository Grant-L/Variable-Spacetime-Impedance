\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=gray!10!white,colframe=black!75!black,title=Design Note 7.1: Transverse Wave Propagation]
    A scalar refractive index $n(r)$ only supports longitudinal (compression) waves. To match LIGO observations of transverse Gravitational Waves (GW), the AVE substrate must support shear stress.
    
    By elevating the moduli to tensors ($\mu_{ij}, \epsilon_{ij}$), the wave equation for a vacuum perturbation $h_{ij}$ becomes:
    \begin{equation}
        (\mu_{ik}\epsilon_{kj} \partial_t^2 - \nabla^2) h_{ij} = 0
    \end{equation}
    In the weak-field limit, the anisotropic shear modulus supports two orthogonal transverse polarizations ($h_{+}$ and $h_{\times}$) propagating at the substrate slew rate $c$.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{Longitudinal Mode:} Damped by the bulk modulus (Dark Energy).
        \item \textbf{Transverse Mode:} Propagates freely as the GW signal.
    \end{itemize}
    This confirms that the "Tensor Refraction" model is formally equivalent to the linearized Einstein Field Equations ($ \Box h_{\mu\nu} = 0$).
    \end{tcolorbox}
