\chapter{Cosmology: Galactic Rotation from Axiom 4}
\label{ch:cosmology_v7}

The most dramatic application of scale invariance closes the
``dark matter problem.''  The MOND acceleration scale
$a_0 \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-10}$~m/s$^2$ is first \textit{derived}:
\begin{resultbox}{Derived MOND Acceleration Scale ($a_0$)}
\begin{equation}
    a_0 = \frac{c \cdot H_\infty}{2\pi}
    \approx 1.07 \times 10^{-10}\;\text{m/s}^2
    \label{eq:a0_derived}
\end{equation}
\end{resultbox}
where $H_\infty$ is the asymptotic Hubble parameter from the derivation
chain (10.7\% below the empirical value).

The lattice mutual inductance $\eta$ at galactic scale is governed
by the \textit{same} saturation operator as particle confinement:
\begin{resultbox}{Saturated Lattice Mutual Inductance}
\begin{equation}
    \eta_{eff}(\dot\gamma) \;=\; \eta_0 \cdot
    \sqrt{1 - \left(\frac{\dot\gamma}{\dot\gamma_{yield}}\right)^{\!2}}
    \label{eq:galactic_inductance}
\end{equation}
\end{resultbox}
When local Newtonian acceleration $g_N$ serves as the saturation
amplitude (with $a_0$ as the yield limit), the drag contribution
becomes:
\begin{resultbox}{Effective Galactic Acceleration (Axiom 4 MOND)}
\begin{equation}
    g_{eff} \;=\; g_N + \sqrt{g_N \cdot a_0}\;
    \sqrt{1 - \frac{g_N}{a_0}}
    \label{eq:saturation_mond}
\end{equation}
\end{resultbox}

\section{Asymptotic Limits}

\begin{itemize}
    \item $g_N \ll a_0$: $g_{eff} \to \sqrt{g_N \cdot a_0}$
          \quad (deep MOND --- flat rotation curves)
    \item $g_N \ge a_0$: The term under the root ($1 - g_N/a_0$) becomes negative, yielding an imaginary inductive drag. In discrete wave mechanics, an imaginary frequency forces the solution to become an \textit{Evanescent Wave}. The drag envelope decays to absolute zero and cannot propagate through the saturated inner-galaxy lattice, leaving only standard, conservative Newtonian gravity:  $g_{eff} = g_N + 0 = g_N$.
\end{itemize}

\section{Regime Identification}
\label{sec:galactic_regimes}

Each galactic radius maps to a specific regime via $r = g_N/a_0$:

\begin{center}

\midrule

\noindent The dark matter problem \textbf{IS} the Regime~I observation of unsaturated vacuum lattice drag.  Inner-galaxy stars at $r \gg 1$ are in Regime~IV: the lattice is fully saturated, drag is zero, orbits are Keplerian.  Outer-galaxy stars at $r \ll 1$ are in Regime~I: the lattice is unsaturated, full drag produces $g_{eff} \to \sqrt{g_N \cdot a_0}$ (flat rotation curves).

\section{Physical Mechanism}

In the outer galaxy where $g_N < a_0$,
the vacuum lattice is in its linear (unsaturated) regime.  Its mutual
inductance $\eta_{eff} \approx \eta_0$ resists differential rotation,
producing an additional radial acceleration that mimics a dark matter
halo.  In the inner galaxy where $g_N > a_0$, the same saturation
operator that drives particle confinement drives
$\eta_{eff} \to 0$~--- the lattice saturates and cannot support
transverse inductive drag.  Orbits become conservative and Keplerian.

\section{The Radial Acceleration Relation (RAR)}
\label{sec:rar}

McGaugh et al.\ (2016) discovered a universal empirical law across 153 SPARC galaxies: the observed acceleration correlates tightly with the baryonic acceleration via a single parameter $a_0$.  The empirical interpolation function is:
\begin{resultbox}{McGaugh Empirical RAR}
\begin{equation}
    g_{obs} = \frac{g_{bar}}{1 - \exp\!\left(-\sqrt{g_{bar}/a_0}\right)}
    \label{eq:rar_mcgaugh}
\end{equation}
\end{resultbox}
The AVE Axiom~4 saturation (Eq.~\ref{eq:saturation_mond}) reproduces the same asymptotes:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $g_{bar} \ll a_0$: both yield $g_{obs} \to \sqrt{g_{bar} \cdot a_0}$ (deep MOND)
    \item $g_{bar} \gg a_0$: both yield $g_{obs} \to g_{bar}$ (Newtonian)
\end{itemize}
The physics engine implements both via \texttt{galactic\_rotation.radial\_acceleration\_relation()}.
