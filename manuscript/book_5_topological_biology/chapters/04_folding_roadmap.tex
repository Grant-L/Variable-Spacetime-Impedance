\section{What Remains: Five Tiers to Full Folding}

The path from equilibrium thermodynamics to atomic-resolution structure prediction has five tiers, each building on the previous.  Each tier is a self-contained first-principles extension --- no fitted parameters.

\subsection{Tier 1: Side-Chain $\chi_1$ Torsion Angles}
\label{sec:tier1_chi}

\paragraph{Current limitation.} Sidechains are encoded as scalar impedance $|Z_\text{topo}|$ with no spatial extent.  Two residues with identical $|Z|$ but different shapes (e.g.\ Leu vs Ile) produce the same fold.

\paragraph{First-principles extension.}
Each residue has 1--4 $\chi$ (chi) torsion angles describing sidechain rotation about covalent bonds.  Adding $\chi_1$ (the first sidechain torsion) gives each residue a directional ``stub'' in 3D space.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{DOF increase}: $2N \to 3N$ (add $\chi_1$ per residue)
    \item \textbf{Bond geometry}: Fixed by covalent radii (Axioms 1--2), same as backbone NERF
    \item \textbf{Steric exclusion}: Extended to include sidechain atoms (Axiom 2)
    \item \textbf{Impedance coupling}: $Y_\text{shunt}$ now depends on sidechain \emph{orientation}, not just $|Z|$
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Expected impact.} Distinguishes Leu/Ile/Val rotamers.  Should improve tertiary contact specificity without any additional physics.

\subsection{Tier 2: PDB Validation (RMSD to Crystal Structures)}
\label{sec:tier2_pdb}

\paragraph{Current limitation.} We predict size ($R_g$) and secondary structure content, but have not compared atom positions to known crystal structures.

\paragraph{Validation protocol.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Fold Villin HP35 (PDB: 1YRF), Trp-cage (1L2Y), GB1 hairpin (2GB1)
    \item Compute backbone C$_\alpha$ RMSD after optimal superposition
    \item Report RMSD, GDT-TS, and TM-score
    \item Compare to baseline (extended chain, ideal helix, random coil)
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Success criterion.} C$_\alpha$ RMSD $< 5$~\AA\ for Villin HP35 would demonstrate that the impedance cascade produces not just the right statistics but the right \emph{topology}.

\subsection{Tier 3: Multi-Chain and Oligomeric Assembly}
\label{sec:tier3_multichain}

\paragraph{Current limitation.} Single-chain only.  Many proteins function as dimers, trimers, or larger assemblies.

\paragraph{First-principles extension.}
Each chain is a separate ABCD cascade terminating at its $N$- and $C$-terminus.  Inter-chain coupling uses the \emph{same} $Y_\text{shunt}$ physics as intra-chain contacts:
\begin{equation}
    Y_{ij}^\text{inter} = \frac{\kappa \cdot m_{ij}}{d_{ij}^2}, \qquad i \in \text{chain A},\; j \in \text{chain B}
\end{equation}
The loss function sums $S_{11}$ over all chains.  Assembly emerges from impedance matching across the interface --- the same physics that drives folding.

\subsection{Tier 4: Folding Kinetics and Pathway Prediction}
\label{sec:tier4_kinetics}

\paragraph{Current limitation.} The optimizer finds a minimum in 5000 gradient steps.  Real proteins fold on $\mu$s--ms timescales through specific pathways.

\paragraph{First-principles extension.}
The ABCD cascade provides a \emph{time-domain} picture: energy injected at one terminus propagates through the backbone as a wave.  The folding pathway is the sequence of impedance-matching events that converts propagating modes to standing modes:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Nucleation}: First stable helix turn forms where $S_{11}$ is locally minimum (highest conjugate match)
    \item \textbf{Propagation}: Impedance-matched regions grow outward (wave front)
    \item \textbf{Collapse}: Long-range $S_{21}$ cross-coupling zips the structure
\end{enumerate}
This predicts folding nucleation sites from the sequence impedance profile alone.

\subsection{Tier 5: Function Prediction from Impedance Landscape}
\label{sec:tier5_function}

\paragraph{Current limitation.} We predict structure but not function.

\paragraph{First-principles extension.}
The impedance mismatch map $S_{11}(i)$ along the backbone identifies:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Active sites}: Regions of high $|S_{11}|$ (impedance discontinuity) that catalyse reactions by concentrating energy
    \item \textbf{Binding sites}: Regions of low $|S_{11}|$ (smooth impedance) that accommodate ligand insertion
    \item \textbf{Allosteric pathways}: Connected regions of moderate $|S_{11}|$ that transmit conformational signals
\end{itemize}
Function prediction requires no additional physics --- only the impedance landscape of the folded structure.
