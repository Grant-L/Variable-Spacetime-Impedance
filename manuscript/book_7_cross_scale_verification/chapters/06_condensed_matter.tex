\section{Superconductivity as Dual Saturation}
\label{sec:superconductivity_duality}

The most striking confirmation of scale invariance is the
\textit{duality} between plasma physics and superconductivity.
Both are instances of the \textit{same} saturation operator acting
on complementary sectors of the impedance:

\begin{center}
\small
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.2}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Property} & \textbf{Plasma ($\varepsilon$-sector)} & \textbf{Superconductor ($\mu$-sector)} \\
\midrule
What saturates      & $\varepsilon_{eff} \to 0$ (capacitor shorts)
                    & $\mu_{eff} \to 0$ (inductor shorts) \\
Saturation argument & $S(E \cdot \ell / V_{snap})$
                    & $S(B / B_c)$ \\
Field expelled      & E-field (Debye screening)
                    & B-field (Meissner effect) \\
Penetration depth   & Skin depth $\delta = c/\omega_p$
                    & London depth $\lambda_L = \sqrt{m^*/\mu_0 n_s e^2}$ \\
Boundary reflection & $\Gamma \to -1$ for $\omega < \omega_p$
                    & $\Gamma \to -1$ for $B < B_c$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{BCS Critical Field = Saturation Factor}

\textbf{Key identity:} The BCS thermodynamic critical field
\begin{equation}
    B_c(T) = B_{c0} \cdot \sqrt{1 - \left(\frac{T}{T_c}\right)^{\!2}}
    \label{eq:bcs_saturation}
\end{equation}
is \textit{identically} \texttt{saturation\_factor(T, T\_c)}.  The
superconducting phase transition is the thermal-sector instance of
Axiom~4 dielectric saturation.  The physics engine implements this
via \texttt{superconductor.critical\_field(T, T\_c, B\_c0)}, which
calls the same universal function used for particle confinement,
FDTD, plasma cutoff, and galactic rotation.
