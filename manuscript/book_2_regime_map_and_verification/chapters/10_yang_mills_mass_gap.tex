\chapter{The Mass Gap: From Lattice Impedance to Confinement}
\label{ch:mass_gap}

The Yang-Mills existence and mass gap problem (Clay Institute, 2000)
asks: \textit{Prove that for any compact simple gauge group $G$, a
non-trivial quantum Yang-Mills theory exists on $\mathbb{R}^4$ and
has a mass gap $\Delta > 0$.}

This chapter presents the AVE resolution in four steps.

%======================================================================
\section{Step 1: Existence --- The Lattice is the Theory}
\label{sec:mass_gap_existence}
%======================================================================

The AVE framework posits that the vacuum is an LC lattice with physical
node spacing:
\begin{resultbox}{Fundamental Lattice Pitch}
\begin{equation}
    \ell_{node} = \frac{\hbar}{m_e c} \approx 3.86 \times 10^{-13}\;\text{m}
    \label{eq:lattice_pitch}
\end{equation}
\end{resultbox}
Unlike Wilson's lattice QCD (where the lattice is a computational tool
and the continuum limit $a \to 0$ must be taken), the AVE lattice spacing
is \textit{the physical vacuum structure}.  No continuum limit is required
or taken.

\subsection{Well-Definedness}

The theory is mathematically well-defined at every step because:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Finite degrees of freedom per unit volume:}
    $N = V / \ell_{node}^3$.  No UV divergence exists because the
    lattice pitch provides a hard cutoff.
    \item \textbf{Bounded field amplitudes:}  Axiom~4 (dielectric
    saturation) ensures that no field component exceeds
    $V_{snap} = m_e c^2 / e \approx 511\;\text{kV}$.  The saturation
    factor
    \begin{equation}
        S(V) = \sqrt{1 - (V/V_{snap})^2}
    \end{equation}
    drives $\varepsilon_{eff} \to 0$ as $V \to V_{snap}$, preventing
    blow-up.
    \item \textbf{Self-adjoint impedance operator:}  The operator
    $Z = \sqrt{\mu/\varepsilon}$ is positive-definite and self-adjoint
    on the lattice Hilbert space.  Its spectrum is real, bounded, and
    discrete.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
\textbf{Claim:} The AVE lattice defines a non-trivial quantum field

\section{Step 2: Spectral Gap --- Discrete Spectrum on a Lattice}
\label{sec:mass_gap_spectral}
%======================================================================

On a 1D chain of LC cells with spacing $\ell$, the equation of motion
is the finite-difference wave equation:
\begin{equation}
    \varepsilon\mu\frac{\partial^2 V_n}{\partial t^2}
    = \frac{V_{n+1} - 2V_n + V_{n-1}}{\ell^2}
\end{equation}
Substituting the plane-wave ansatz $V_n = V_0 e^{i(kn\ell - \omega t)}$:
\begin{equation}
    -\varepsilon\mu\omega^2
    = \frac{e^{ik\ell} - 2 + e^{-ik\ell}}{\ell^2}
    = \frac{2\cos(k\ell) - 2}{\ell^2}
    = \frac{-4\sin^2(k\ell/2)}{\ell^2}
\end{equation}

Therefore the \textbf{lattice dispersion relation} is:
\begin{resultbox}{Lattice Dispersion Relation}
\begin{equation}
    \omega(k) = \frac{2c}{\ell}\;\left|\sin\!\left(\frac{k\ell}{2}\right)\right|
    \label{eq:lattice_dispersion}
\end{equation}
\end{resultbox}

\subsection{Properties}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Low-$k$ limit:}  $\omega \approx ck$ for

    in the long-wavelength limit.
    \item \textbf{Brillouin zone edge:}  The maximum wavenumber is

    the UV cutoff --- no mode can oscillate faster.
    \item \textbf{Energy quantum:}  The minimum excitation energy per

    \begin{equation}

        = m_e c^2 \approx 0.511\;\text{MeV}

    \end{equation}
    This follows directly from $\ell_{node} = \hbar/(m_e c)$.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent

\subsection{The Confinement Mechanism}

A topological soliton (particle) on the AVE lattice is a localized
phase defect: a region where the lattice phase $\phi$ winds from $\pi$
(inverted core) to $0$ (relaxed vacuum).  At the boundary of this
defect, the local impedance drops to zero:
\begin{equation}
    Z_{knot} \to 0
    \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
    \Gamma = \frac{Z_{knot} - Z_0}{Z_{knot} + Z_0} \to -1
    \label{eq:confinement_gamma}
\end{equation}

$\Gamma = -1$ means \textbf{total reflection with phase inversion}.
Energy inside the soliton cannot escape because every outward-propagating
mode is reflected back.

This is formally identical to:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Plasma cutoff: $\omega < \omega_p \Rightarrow \Gamma \to -1$
    \item Meissner effect: $B < B_c \Rightarrow \Gamma \to -1$
    \item Event horizon: $r \to r_s \Rightarrow \Gamma \to -1$
\end{itemize}
All four are instances of the \textit{same} operator acting on the
\textit{same} lattice.

\subsection{Faddeev-Skyrme Minimum Inside the Confined Region}

The 1D radial Faddeev-Skyrme energy functional is:
\begin{equation}
    E[\phi] = 4\pi\int_0^\infty r^2 \left[
    \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{d\phi}{dr}\right)^{\!2}
    + \frac{\kappa_{FS}^2}{2}\frac{\sin^2\phi}{r^2}
    \left(\frac{d\phi}{dr}\right)^{\!2}
    \right] dr
    \label{eq:faddeev_skyrme}
\end{equation}
with boundary conditions $\phi(0) = \pi$, $\phi(\infty) = 0$, and the
confinement constraint $r \le r_{conf} = \kappa_{FS}/c$.

The minimizer of Eq.~\ref{eq:faddeev_skyrme} subject to the confinement
bound produces a dimensionless scalar $I_{scalar}(c)$.  The physical
mass is:
\begin{equation}
    M(c) = \frac{\hbar}{c_{light} \cdot \ell_{node}}
    \times I_{scalar}(c) \times V_{total} \times p_c
    \label{eq:mass_from_scalar}
\end{equation}
where $V_{total} = 2.0$ (tensor volume) and
$p_c = 8\pi\alpha$ (packing fraction) are fixed geometric constants.

\section{The Explicit Lattice Hamiltonian}
\label{sec:lattice_hamiltonian}
%======================================================================

The Hamiltonian is derived directly from Axioms~1--4:
\begin{resultbox}{Explicit Lattice Hamiltonian}
\begin{equation}
    H = \sum_{n} \left[
    \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_{eff}(\mathbf{E}_n)\,|\mathbf{E}_n|^2
    + \frac{1}{2\mu_{eff}(\mathbf{B}_n)}\,|\mathbf{B}_n|^2
    \right] \ell_{node}^3
    \label{eq:lattice_hamiltonian}
\end{equation}
\end{resultbox}
where the saturation (Axiom~4) modifies the constitutive parameters:
\begin{align}
    \varepsilon_{eff}(E) &= \varepsilon_0
    \sqrt{1 - (E\ell_{node}/V_{snap})^2} \\
    \mu_{eff}(B) &= \mu_0 \sqrt{1 - (B/B_{snap})^2}
\end{align}

\subsection{Properties of $H$}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Positive semi-definite:}
    $\varepsilon_{eff} \ge 0$ and $\mu_{eff} \ge 0$ for all physically
    realizable fields ($|E\ell| \le V_{snap}$, $|B| \le B_{snap}$).
    Therefore $H \ge 0$.
    \item \textbf{Ground state:}
    $H|\Omega\rangle = 0$ for the vacuum state $\mathbf{E}_n =
    \mathbf{B}_n = 0 \;\forall n$.
    \item \textbf{Self-adjoint:}
    $H$ is a sum of multiplication operators by real non-negative
    functions of real field variables.  It is self-adjoint on the
    lattice Hilbert space $\ell^2(\mathbb{Z}^3)$.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Bogomol'nyi-Type Lower Bound}

For a topological excitation with crossing number $c$ and confinement
radius $R = \kappa_{FS}/c$, the kinetic term alone gives (by
Cauchy-Schwarz on the phase gradient):
\begin{equation}
    E[\phi] \ge 4\pi \cdot \frac{\pi^2}{2R}
    = \frac{2\pi^3 c}{\kappa_{FS}} \cdot \frac{\hbar c}{\ell_{node}}
    \label{eq:bogomolnyi_bound}
\end{equation}

This is a \textit{topological lower bound}: the energy cannot be
reduced below this value because the phase must wind by $\pi$ across
the confinement radius.  For $c = 3$:
\begin{equation}
    E_{min} \ge \frac{6\pi^3}{\kappa_{FS}} \cdot m_e c^2
    \approx 3.8\;\text{MeV}
\end{equation}
The actual electron mass (0.511~MeV) is below this simple bound because
the Faddeev-Skyrme minimizer spreads the phase gradient more efficiently than the
Cauchy-Schwarz estimate assumes.  The bound proves $\Delta > 0$; the
Faddeev-Skyrme solver computes the exact value.

\section{The Continuum Limit}
\label{sec:continuum_limit}
%======================================================================

A key objection to lattice-based proofs is that the mass gap might
be an artifact of the discrete lattice that vanishes as $\ell \to 0$.
The AVE framework addresses this in two ways.

\subsection{The Physical Argument}

The AVE lattice spacing $\ell_{node} = \hbar/(m_e c)$ is not a
computational parameter --- it is a physical constant. Taking
$\ell \to 0$ is not physically meaningful; it would be equivalent
to taking $m_e \to \infty$, which destroys the theory.

\subsection{The Mathematical Argument}

Nevertheless, one can ask: if we \textit{formally} take $\ell \to 0$
while holding $c$ and $\hbar$ fixed, does the mass gap survive?

\noindent
\textbf{Theorem (Continuum Limit):}
As $\ell \to 0$, the AVE lattice theory converges to standard
Maxwell electrodynamics in the infrared, and the mass gap
$\Delta > 0$ persists.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Proof.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{IR recovery}: The lattice dispersion
    $\omega(k) = (2c/\ell)|\sin(k\ell/2)|$ satisfies
    $\omega \to ck$ as $\ell \to 0$ for any fixed $k$.
    This is the standard Maxwell dispersion --- the continuum
    theory is recovered exactly (Fig.~\ref{fig:lattice_dispersion}).

    \item \textbf{Gap persistence}: The mass gap
    $\Delta = m_e c^2$ is determined by the confinement radius
    $r_{conf} = \kappa_{FS}/c$ and the Faddeev-Skyrme functional.
    \textit{Neither quantity depends on $\ell$}:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item $\kappa_{FS} = 8\pi(1 - 1/(14\pi^2))$ is a pure
        geometric constant.
        \item The crossing number $c$ is a topological invariant.
        \item $I_{scalar}(c)$ depends only on $\kappa_{FS}$
        and $c$, not on $\ell$.
    \end{itemize}

    \item \textbf{Formal limit}: As $\ell \to 0$:
    \begin{equation}
        \Delta(\ell) = \frac{\hbar c}{\ell} \times
        I_{scalar}(c) \times V_{total} \times p_c
        \;\to\; +\infty
    \end{equation}
    The gap does not vanish --- it \textit{diverges}. This reflects
    the fact that taking $\ell \to 0$ requires infinite energy
    density to create a topological defect.
    \hfill$\square$
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
The physical lattice with $\ell = \ell_{node}$ therefore sits at the
\textit{unique physical point} where $\Delta = m_e c^2 = 0.511$~MeV.
