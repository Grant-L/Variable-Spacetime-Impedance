\noindent\textbf{Applied Vacuum Engineering: Volume VI} \\
This document presents the experimental apparatus designs for direct laboratory verification of the AVE framework.

\begin{abstract}
    The preceding volumes derive the complete AVE framework from first principles. This volume translates those predictions into concrete, buildable hardware.

    \textbf{Volume VI: Experimental Hardware} details the PONDER (Pulsed Oscillatory Non-linear Dielectric Excitation Rig) family of experiments and the HOPF-01 chiral antenna, each designed to probe a specific regime of the universal saturation kernel:
    \begin{equation}
        S(V) = \sqrt{1 - \left(\frac{V}{V_\text{yield}}\right)^{\!2}}, \qquad V_\text{yield} = \sqrt{\alpha} \cdot \frac{m_e c^2}{e} \approx 43.65\;\text{kV}
    \end{equation}
    The programme spans high-voltage VHF ceramic drives (PONDER-01), wire-stitched torus knot antennas measuring chiral $S_{11}$ anomalies (HOPF-01), and DC-biased quartz resonators probing dielectric saturation onset (PONDER-05). Every experimental design parameter --- drive voltage, frequency, geometry, and expected signal level --- is derived from the axioms with zero empirical curve-fitting.
\end{abstract}
