\section{Resolution of the Flyby Anomaly}
\label{sec:cassini_anomaly}

The "Flyby Anomaly" refers to the unexplained velocity shifts ($\Delta v \approx 1-10$ mm/s) observed in spacecraft (Galileo, NEAR, Cassini) performing Earth gravity-assist maneuvers.

While General Relativity predicts "Frame Dragging" (Lense-Thirring), the effect is negligible ($10^{-10}$ m/s). VSI resolves this by identifying the vacuum's \textbf{Viscous Coupling} to the metric strain rate.

\subsection{The Strain Rate Hypothesis}
We identify the "Lattice Density" not as a static scalar, but as the \textbf{Metric Strain Rate} ($\dot{\epsilon}$).
\begin{equation}
    \dot{\epsilon} = \frac{g}{c} = \frac{GM}{r^2 c} \quad [s^{-1}]
\end{equation}
The viscous force exerted by the lattice on a spacecraft is proportional to the Fine Structure Constant ($\alpha$) and this strain rate:
\begin{equation}
    F_{viscous} = \alpha \cdot \dot{\epsilon} \cdot (\vec{v}_{lattice} - \vec{v}_{ship})
\end{equation}
