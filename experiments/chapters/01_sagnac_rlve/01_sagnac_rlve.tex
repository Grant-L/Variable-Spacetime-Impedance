\section{The Sagnac Phase Shift in a Bingham-Plastic Fluid}
We define the tabletop Sagnac Rotational Lattice Viscosity Experiment (Sagnac-RLVE). Unlike standard General Relativistic frame-dragging (Lense-Thirring), which evaluates to zero for non-gravitational laboratory masses, the AVE framework predicts a measurable kinematic entrainment.



Given the derived kinematic viscosity of the unperturbed lattice:

$$\nu_{vac} = \alpha c l_{node} \approx 8.45 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}^2/\text{s}$$

The expected optical phase shift ($\Delta\phi_{Sagnac}$) for a fiber-optic loop of area $A$ rotating at an angular velocity $\Omega$ in the presence of a localized, high-shear boundary layer evaluates to:

$$\Delta\phi_{Sagnac} = \frac{8\pi A \Omega}{\lambda c} \left( 1 + f_{drag}(\nu_{vac}, \tau_{yield}) \right)$$
