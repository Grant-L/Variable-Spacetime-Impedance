\section{Helicity as Charge}
\label{sec:helicity_charge}

In Chapter 2, we identified Mass as "Inductive Energy" ($E=\frac{1}{2}LI^2$). \citestart Here, we identify Electric Charge ($q$) as \textbf{Topological Winding Number ($N$)}\cite{1068}\citeend.

The phase $\theta$ of the vacuum potential winds around a singularity in the lattice. \citestart In the discrete manifold $M_A$, this winding must be an integer to prevent topological tearing\cite{1069}\citeend:
\begin{equation}
    q \propto \oint \nabla \theta \cdot dl = 2\pi N
\end{equation}

\subsection{Chirality and Sign}
The orientation of this twist relative to the global vacuum grain ($\mathbf{\Omega}_{vac}$) determines the charge polarity:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Negative Charge ($e^-$):} A Left-Handed Twist ($N = -1$).
    \item \textbf{Positive Charge ($e^+$):} A Right-Handed Twist ($N = +1$).
\end{itemize}
